The Truly Minimalist Newspaper

And I am sure that I never read any memorable news in a newspaper. -Henry David Thoreau

Generally, there are two ways to get the news:

  1. From talking to friends and acquaintances.
  2. From ad-supported organizations that understand that the best way to make money is to report lots of the most negative, emotion-stirring things (whether or not we really need to know them), as this best gets people's attention.

In response to this, some people have made the conscious choice to limit their exposure to news media. Some may even realize that the number of news events they really needed to know about annually could generally be counted on one hand.

For people who feel this way and don't want to be entirely reliant on their friends to learn about major events or how society is doing, there is the Truly Minimalist Newspaper.

How it Works

This newspaper is delivered in the form of an email newsletter. Subscribers will get emails whenever something truly newsworthy happens, as well as a tri-annual short summary of what is going on in society in general. These summaries are no more than a few paragraphs.

To see what kind of stuff gets sent out, take a look at the history.

The Threshold

It is difficult to determine where to draw the line about what one needs to know to be minimally informed. I will be using the threshold that works best for me:

Something is newsworthy if it would directly and substantially affect the life of someone living in the United States or Canada. This should happen fewer than five times per year.
Obviously there are local events that could affect someone, but I'm thinking about a generic American or Canadian. People will need a different source of information for events that are only of local or specialized interest.

Who this is For

You might be interested in this if you are a minimalist, a follower of the Low Information Diet, a fan of simple living, a member of the Attention Resistance, or just a conscious consumer of media.
